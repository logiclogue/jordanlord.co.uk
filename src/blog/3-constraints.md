I've been a builder and software engineer since my days as a teen. I've always
enjoyed tinkering around, especially with retro technology, simulations, and
game engines. Founding Footium back in 2020 has helped me understand how
businesses function, how to fundraise, how to work in teams, what it really
takes to scale businesses. Seeing friends and family on the same path, I'm
writing this small manifesto to help organise my thoughts for the coming year.
I'm a believer in constraints as an enabler for creativity. Constraints help us
collapse the search space, and figure out innovative solutions to problems.

## Can it totally and complete be explained in a one pager?

Can your idea or project be totally and completely explained in one page of
writing? And if it can, write one. Use it as the north star for your project.
The idea is that this can be used as a memo for investors, contributors, team
members, friends, or family. It's a manifesto of sorts. Often when building
projects, there are contention points, it's difficult to know what battles to
pick. If it's not in the one pager, then it's either not worth fighting over, or
the one pager aught to be amended to include the thing. Not only is a one pager
useful for collaboration, it's useful for organising your own thoughts.

At Footium, we switched our focus from product to growth recently. To achieve
this, I sought to implement a growth hacking mindset. Sean Ellis wrote an
excellent book called 'Hacking Growth', which outlines his process and
principles for running a growth hacking progress. To implement this process at
Footium, I wrote a one pager which outlined the changes clearly. The one pager
has been instrumental for maintaining alignment with the team as we have
progressed through the process.

## Does it have a unique technical edge?

Does your passion project have a unique technical edge? Even if you think you
may have something unique, it's probably been done before. As a society we're
constantly re-investing the wheel. This constraint goes to the core of what
you're building, it's asking a few questions. It's asking, are you developing a
technology that can be applied to something else moving forwards? On the
surface, your product might seem some fairly standard consumer focused thing,
but you could be developing some deep tech that helps accelerate and support
that in some way. This helps you pivot into something else when it's time to
move on. It keeps your mind fresh. This can evolve over time, your product or
business is likely to pivot in focus and your unique technical edge may no
longer be relevant.

I've been on a winding path with this when it came to Footium. At first, the
unique technical angle was developing off-chain deterministic engines where the
state could be verified on chain. Then it became building deterministic systems
that inherently encode for skill instead of randomness, where the simulation
engine it's based on potluck, e.g. you're able to discover all of the variables
that make up a game and they coherently make sense in the context of the
simulation, but aren't the determining factor. Now where I'm at, is utilising
agentic AI to dig out deep analytical insights that will accelerate the growth
of the business.
